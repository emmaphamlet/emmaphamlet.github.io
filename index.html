About

Student • Data analysis at the intersection of tech + well-being

I’m Emma — an Informatics and Psychology student interested in how technology and everyday digital habits relate to well-being. I enjoy turning messy real-world datasets into clear insights through data cleaning, visualization, and statistical testing.

Recently, I analyzed survey data on music listening behaviors (genre preference, listening time, listening while working, and musician status) to explore how these behaviors relate to self-reported anxiety and depression. I focus on communicating results clearly and acknowledging limitations like self-report bias, convenience sampling, and correlation vs. causation.
